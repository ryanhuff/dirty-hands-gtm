# Quotivity — Master Knowledge Base

## Company Overview

### Vision

Confident, accurate quoting that keeps complex sales flowing in HubSpot.

### Mission

Simplify complex quoting so sales, RevOps, and finance teams can create accurate quotes quickly without juggling extra tools.

### Boilerplate

Quotivity is a HubSpot-native quoting solution built for companies that have outgrown spreadsheets and HubSpot's default CPQ but don't need the cost and complexity of enterprise tools like DealHub. We give sales, RevOps, and finance teams native rules, price books, and approvals so reps quote fast and finance trusts the numbers. Quotivity serves nearly 100 customers, with deep traction in SaaS and manufacturing.

### What We Do (Plain Language)

We make complex quoting work inside HubSpot. If your sales team deals with bundled products, tiered pricing, product interdependencies, or approval workflows, HubSpot's built-in quoting can't keep up and spreadsheets fall apart. Quotivity adds the pricing rules, product configuration, and approval logic your team needs — without pulling anyone out of HubSpot. Reps build quotes faster, finance controls discounting, and management gets visibility into what's being quoted.

### Stage and Business Model

- **Stage:** Early revenue, ~100 customers
- **Business model:** SaaS subscription, licensed per salesperson producing and approving quotes
- **Average TCV:** ~$10K USD and growing
- **Primary segments:** SaaS companies with complex pricing models; manufacturing (configure-to-order, assemble-to-order), dealers, and distributors
- **Platform:** Exclusive to HubSpot. Requires Sales Hub Professional.

### HubSpot-Native Advantage

Being native to HubSpot means customers use all other HubSpot platform capabilities without friction. Everything upstream and downstream of the quote — contacts, deals, workflows, reporting, integrations — stays connected. No data sync issues, no separate login, no parallel system to maintain.

## Strategic Positioning

### Narrative

Before Quotivity, companies with any degree of complexity in their quoting process had two bad options. They could buy a solution like DealHub — expensive, overcomplicated, and never fully integrated with HubSpot. Or they could rely on Excel spreadsheets that go stale the moment someone saves them, are difficult to maintain, and impossible to distribute reliably.

Both paths led to the same place: slow and inaccurate quoting, revenue leakage from uncontrolled discounting and missed line items, and zero visibility into what reps were promising customers.

Quotivity exists for the companies in the middle. Teams that need more than HubSpot's default CPQ but don't need (or can't justify) an enterprise quoting platform. We bring complex pricing, product configuration, and approval workflows natively into HubSpot — so the quoting process lives where the sales team already works.

### Point of View Statements

**POV 1: Quoting belongs inside your CRM, not in a separate tool.**
The moment you export to a spreadsheet or switch to another app, you lose data integrity and visibility. Every disconnected step is a chance for errors to compound and data to go stale.

**POV 2: Complex quoting shouldn't require a complex tool.**
Solutions like DealHub solve the problem by adding layers of configuration most mid-market teams never need. The result is long implementations, high costs, and tools that only one person on the team knows how to manage.

**POV 3: Revenue leakage isn't a pricing problem — it's a process problem.**
When reps quote from outdated spreadsheets or disconnected tools, errors compound silently. Missed line items, inconsistent discounting, and untracked changes erode margin deal by deal. Fix the process and the revenue follows.

### Urgency Drivers

- Continuing to rely on disconnected processes means ongoing revenue leakage — every quarter without a fix is money lost.
- Limited visibility into what's being promised to customers creates risk that compounds as deal volume grows.
- Implementing Quotivity means reps focus on discovering customer needs and negotiating deals instead of battling broken quoting processes.
- Management gets the data needed for strategic decisions instead of operating on incomplete information.

## Product Capabilities

### Core Features

| Feature | What It Does | Why It Matters |
|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Price Books | Centralized pricing with rules, tiers, and volume logic | One source of truth for pricing. Reps can't quote stale numbers. |
| Product Configuration | Define product interdependencies, required add-ons, and valid combinations | Prevents invalid quotes. Reps don't need to memorize what goes with what. |
| Approval Workflows | Route quotes for approval based on discount thresholds, deal size, or product rules | Finance controls discounting without reviewing every quote manually. |
| Quote Templates | Customizable, branded quote documents generated natively in HubSpot | RevOps manages templates centrally. Reps stop designing PDFs themselves. |
| Policy Enforcement | Rules that govern what can be quoted, at what price, with what terms | Guardrails that let reps move fast while staying within policy. |
| Complex Pricing | Support for bundled pricing, tiered pricing, formula-based pricing, and product-dependent pricing | Handles the pricing models that spreadsheets and HubSpot CPQ can't. |
| Discount Controls | Set discount limits by role, product, or deal type with automatic escalation | Reduces revenue leakage from uncontrolled discounting. |

### Key Terminology

- **CPQ:** Configure, Price, Quote — the process of configuring products, applying correct pricing, and generating a quote document.
- **Price Book:** A centralized set of pricing rules that governs what products cost under what conditions.
- **Product Configuration:** Defining which products can be sold together, which are required, and which are incompatible.
- **Approval Workflow:** An automated routing process that sends quotes to the right approver based on predefined rules.
- **Policy Enforcement:** System-level rules that prevent quotes from being sent without meeting defined criteria.
- **Revenue Leakage:** Lost margin from discounting errors, missed line items, or untracked changes that accumulate across deals.
- **Quote Velocity:** The speed at which quotes move from creation to customer delivery.

**Role context:** Owns quota and sales team performance. Accountable for deal velocity and revenue targets. Frustrated by administrative overhead that pulls them away from strategic work.

**Buying psychology:** Cares about rep productivity and deal speed. Wants to remove friction from the quoting process so reps spend time selling, not building quotes. Needs to see direct impact on quote velocity and accuracy.

**Common objections:**

#### 2. RevOps Leader

**Role context:** Owns the quoting process, templates, and policy enforcement. Responsible for making the sales tech stack work together. First to feel the pain of HubSpot CPQ's limitations.

**Buying psychology:** Wants control over templating, policies, and pricing in one place. Pragmatic about tools — needs it to actually reduce process complexity, not add another system to manage.

**Common objections:**
- Implementation burden. Depending on catalog size and policy complexity, the setup work could land heavily on their shoulders.

#### 3. Finance / Controller

**Role context:** Cares about margin integrity, discount governance, and reliable reporting on what's being quoted. Often the person manually reviewing every quote to catch discounting issues.

**Buying psychology:** Risk-focused. Wants to trust that quotes going out the door are accurate and within policy. Values controls and reporting over speed.

**Pain points (in their language):**
- "I have no ability to control discounting beyond reviewing every quote that goes out."
- "I can't reliably report on discounting patterns and product combinations."
- "I'm doing busy work — reviewing quotes just to keep discounting under control."

**Decision criteria:** Discount controls and escalation rules, reporting on margins and discounting, quote accuracy and integrity.

**Common objections:**
- Cost.

## Communication Guidelines

### Voice and Tone

**We sound like:** A knowledgeable peer who has spent too many hours debugging broken quoting processes — direct, specific, lightly humorous when the situation calls for it. We write for sales, RevOps, and finance at the same time. We respect complexity without being complicated.

**We don't sound like:** An enterprise vendor. A startup that thinks "AI-powered" is a positioning statement. A HubSpot blog post that's all enthusiasm and no specifics.

### Style Rules

1. **Sentences:** Short to medium. One idea per sentence.
2. **Data:** Use concrete examples and simple math when possible. "29 days to 6 days" beats "significant reduction."
3. **Jargon:** Only standard HubSpot and quoting terms. Define anything uncommon.
4. **Inclusivity:** Write for sales, RevOps, and finance at the same time. If a sentence only makes sense to one audience, rewrite it.
5. **Humor:** Light, situational, never at a customer's expense.
6. **Lead with the problem, not the product.** The reader should nod before they see our name.
7. **Show, don't label.** Don't call something "powerful" or "flexible." Describe what it does and let the reader draw the conclusion.

### Reference Examples

- **Tagline:** Quotes with guardrails, inside HubSpot.
- **One-liner:** Native rules, price books, and approvals so reps quote fast and finance trusts the numbers.
- **Email subject:** Make HubSpot quotes behave.
- **UI helper text:** You're missing a required input. Add it and we'll finish the quote.

### Anti-Patterns

- **Feature dumping:** Listing capabilities without connecting them to outcomes. Every feature mention needs a "which means..." for the reader.
- **HubSpot bashing:** We complement HubSpot, we don't trash it. Position as an upgrade, not a replacement.
- **Complexity theater:** Making quoting sound harder than it is to justify the product. Our job is to make it simpler, and our content should reflect that.
- **Audience exclusion:** Writing only for RevOps nerds or only for sales leaders. Every piece should be accessible to the full buying committee.

## Case Studies Summary

### Case Study 1: Aptarro (Healthcare SaaS)

- **Industry:** Healthcare SaaS
- **Challenge:** Completely unstructured quoting process. Sales reps manually entered line items in HubSpot and referenced pricing from spreadsheets saved to their desktops. No approval workflows, pricing varied wildly between deals, and downstream systems (accounting, legal, operations) constantly reconciled conflicting data.
- **Solution:** Implemented Quotivity alongside a CLM system. Rebuilt their entire price book from the ground up to establish consistent pricing logic across products.
- **Results:**
  - 79% reduction in negotiation time (from 29 days to 6 days average)
  - Standardized pricing across all reps and partners
  - Reduced unnecessary discounting
  - Fewer errors and rework throughout the sales cycle
  - Deal review calls shortened or eliminated entirely
- **Key quote:** "There was an Excel pricing table somewhere that they saved to their desktop. It was very freeform."

### Case Study 2: True North (Managed IT & Cloud Services)

- **Industry:** Managed IT and cloud services (healthcare focus)
- **Challenge:** Complex bundled service quoting with significant margin leakage. Line items frequently missed during quote creation. Pricing estimated rather than calculated. Inability to unbundle agreements when customer needs changed. Manual processes spread across Excel, Word, and ConnectWise Sell.
- **Solution:** Implemented Quotivity for formula-based pricing, bundle management with component visibility, and integrated approval workflows.
- **Results:**
  - 30% margin recovery
  - Improved speed to close, especially for existing customers
  - Reduced revenue leakage from untracked service changes
  - Fewer missed components in bundled quotes
- **Key quote:** "Our guys just don't miss things now."

### vs. Mobileforce

- **Their strength:** Good for field service operating in remote areas.
- **Their weakness:** Quoting outside of HubSpot. Limited support for complex pricing. No support for complex bundles and configuration.
- **Our position:** Not appropriate for companies committed to HubSpot as their CRM.

### vs. GleanQuote

- **Their strength:** Good all-purpose quoting solution.
- **Their weakness:** Quoting outside of HubSpot. Limited support for complex pricing. No support for complex bundles and configuration.
- **Our position:** Not appropriate for companies committed to HubSpot as their CRM.

### vs. Excel Spreadsheets (Status Quo)

- **Their strength:** Flexible. Everyone knows how to use them.
- **Their weakness:** Disconnected from everything. Best case is answers you need to transfer to another system. Difficult to maintain and distribute. No version control, no approval logic, no reporting.

### vs. ERP Quoting (Status Quo)

- **Their strength:** It's an existing system.
- **Their weakness:** It's not where the sales team works — they work in the CRM. Generally difficult and expensive to customize and maintain.

### How We Win

We win on HubSpot-native depth and speed-to-value. Competitors either live outside HubSpot (Mobileforce, GleanQuote, Excel, ERP) or are too expensive and complex for mid-market teams (DealHub). HubSpot's own CPQ can't handle complex pricing and configuration. We're the only option that is both deeply native to HubSpot and purpose-built for complex quoting.

### Where We Lose (Honestly)

We lose to DealHub in accounts that need extreme complexity beyond what mid-market teams typically require, or when the buying decision isn't tied to HubSpot adoption. We handle this by staying focused on the HubSpot ecosystem and the companies for whom HubSpot is the center of their sales process.
