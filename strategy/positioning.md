# Quotivity — Positioning

---

## Narrative Arc

### Act 1: The Old World

Companies with complex quoting have been stuck with two bad options. Enterprise tools like DealHub handle the complexity but cost a fortune, take months to implement, and never fully integrate with HubSpot. The alternative is spreadsheets: flexible, familiar, and disconnected from everything. Reps save pricing tables to their desktops. Every quote looks different. Every quote needs manual approval because nobody trusts the numbers.

The result: slow quoting, inaccurate pricing, and revenue leaking out of every deal. Sales management spends hours reviewing quotes that should have been right the first time. Finance discovers discounting problems after the fact. RevOps patches workarounds that break with the next product catalog change.

### Act 2: The Disruption

HubSpot became the CRM of choice for growing companies. But as these companies scaled, their quoting needs outpaced what HubSpot's native CPQ could handle. Bundled products, tiered pricing, product interdependencies, approval workflows, discount controls — none of it worked natively. Companies that chose HubSpot for its simplicity found themselves bolting on complex, disconnected quoting systems that undermined the reason they picked HubSpot in the first place.

Meanwhile, the manufacturing sector — configure-to-order and assemble-to-order companies, dealers, and distributors — started adopting HubSpot as their CRM. These teams brought quoting complexity that no HubSpot-native tool was built to handle.

### Act 3: The New Reality

Quotivity brings complex quoting natively into HubSpot. Price books, product configuration, approval workflows, discount controls, and branded quote templates — all inside the CRM where the sales team already works. Reps build accurate quotes in minutes instead of hours. Finance sets discount limits that enforce themselves. RevOps manages templates and policies from one place. Management sees what's being quoted in real time.

The intelligence gap between "what HubSpot can do" and "what our quoting process requires" no longer forces a choice between an enterprise tool and a spreadsheet.

## Point of View Statements

**POV 1: Quoting belongs inside your CRM, not in a separate tool.**
The moment you export to a spreadsheet or switch to another app, you lose data integrity and visibility. Every disconnected step is a chance for errors to compound and data to go stale. Everything upstream and downstream of the quote — contacts, deals, workflows, reporting — should stay connected.

**POV 2: Complex quoting shouldn't require a complex tool.**
Solutions like DealHub solve the problem by adding layers of configuration most mid-market teams never need. The result is long implementations, high costs, and tools that only one person on the team knows how to manage. Complexity in the product catalog should not mean complexity in the tool.

**POV 3: Revenue leakage isn't a pricing problem — it's a process problem.**
When reps quote from outdated spreadsheets or disconnected tools, errors compound silently. Missed line items, inconsistent discounting, and untracked changes erode margin deal by deal. Aptarro had reps quoting from spreadsheets saved to their desktops. True North was estimating prices instead of calculating them. Fix the process and the revenue follows.

## Differentiators

### Differentiator 1: HubSpot-Native and HubSpot-Exclusive

- **What it is:** Quotivity is built inside HubSpot and exclusively for HubSpot. Not a bolt-on integration. Not a separate platform with a sync.
- **Why it matters:** Everything upstream and downstream of the quote stays connected. Contacts, deals, workflows, reporting, integrations — no data sync issues, no separate login, no parallel system to maintain. Reps don't leave their CRM.
- **Proof point:** Quotivity is compatible with everything in the HubSpot platform. Customers use HubSpot workflows, reporting, and integrations with Quotivity data natively.

### Differentiator 2: Complex Pricing and Configuration Without Enterprise Complexity

- **What it is:** Price books, tiered pricing, formula-based pricing, bundled pricing, product interdependencies, and required add-ons — all configurable by RevOps without developer resources.
- **Why it matters:** This is the gap HubSpot's native CPQ can't fill and the problem DealHub oversolves. Mid-market teams get the pricing logic they need without the 6-month implementation or six-figure contract.
- **Proof point:** True North recovered 30% margin using Quotivity's formula-based pricing and bundle management. They had been estimating prices manually because their previous tools couldn't handle the calculation.

### Differentiator 3: Guardrails That Replace Administrative Approvals

- **What it is:** Discount controls, policy enforcement, and approval workflows that prevent bad quotes from going out — without requiring manual review of every quote.
- **Why it matters:** Sales management and finance get their time back. Reps move faster because they're not waiting for approvals on quotes that were correct in the first place. Approvals that do trigger are strategically relevant, not administrative.
- **Proof point:** Aptarro reduced negotiation time by 79% (29 days to 6 days). Deal review calls were shortened or eliminated because the quoting guardrails made manual checking unnecessary.

### Differentiator 4: Speed to Value

- **What it is:** Implementation measured in weeks, not months. Teams go from purchase to quoting quickly.
- **Why it matters:** Companies choosing between Quotivity and DealHub are often facing an upcoming busy season or a specific deadline. A 6-month implementation isn't viable. Quotivity delivers value before the next quarter.
- **Proof point:** Positioning against DealHub's long implementation cycles. Quotivity's focus on HubSpot-native architecture eliminates the integration complexity that drives enterprise implementation timelines.

## Value Propositions

### Value Proposition 1: Faster, More Accurate Quotes

- **Target pain:** VP of Sales: "Quoting is slow and inaccurate. Reps jump between spreadsheets and outside systems."
- **Promise:** Reps build accurate quotes inside HubSpot in minutes, not hours.
- **Mechanism:** Centralized price books, product configuration rules, and branded templates eliminate manual lookup, spreadsheet referencing, and DIY PDF design.
- **Proof:** Aptarro cut negotiation time by 79% (29 days to 6 days) after implementing Quotivity.

### Value Proposition 2: Margin Protection Through Process

- **Target pain:** Finance: "No ability to control discounting beyond reviewing every quote."
- **Promise:** Discount controls and policy enforcement that protect margins without manual review of every quote.
- **Mechanism:** Discount limits by role, product, or deal type with automatic escalation. Policies that prevent quotes from being sent without meeting defined criteria.
- **Proof:** True North recovered 30% margin after implementing Quotivity. Missed line items and estimated pricing were replaced by formula-based calculations and bundle management with component visibility.

### Value Proposition 3: RevOps Control Without Constant Maintenance

- **Target pain:** RevOps: "Quote templates are painful to customize. Policy enforcement is almost completely lacking."
- **Promise:** Manage templates, policies, and pricing from one place — inside HubSpot.
- **Mechanism:** Customizable quote templates, centralized policy management, and pricing rules that RevOps controls without developer resources or fighting HubSpot's native limitations.
- **Proof:** Quotivity replaces the template and policy gaps in HubSpot's native CPQ that force RevOps into spreadsheet workarounds.

### Value Proposition 4: Visibility Into What's Being Quoted

- **Target pain:** VP of Sales: "I can't see what's being quoted until the deal closes."
- **Promise:** Real-time visibility into quoting activity, discounting patterns, and product combinations.
- **Mechanism:** Because Quotivity is native to HubSpot, all quoting data flows into HubSpot's native reporting and dashboards. No separate analytics tool or manual data export.
- **Proof:** Aptarro eliminated the information gap that came from reps quoting from disconnected spreadsheets. Management gained visibility into pricing consistency across reps and partners.
