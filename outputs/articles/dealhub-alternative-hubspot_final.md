# DealHub Alternative for HubSpot: Native CPQ Compared

A DealHub alternative for HubSpot is a Configure, Price, Quote (CPQ) tool that handles [complex quoting](INTERNAL_LINK: hubspot-cpq-complex-pricing) while working natively inside HubSpot rather than as a bolt-on integration. The key distinction is whether the tool uses HubSpot's native objects (quotes, line items, subscriptions) or creates its own custom objects that require separate administration and introduce data sync issues. For mid-market teams committed to HubSpot, this architectural difference determines whether your CPQ makes HubSpot more useful or less.

## Why DealHub Is the Wrong Fit for Most HubSpot Teams

DealHub is enterprise CPQ. It supports Salesforce, HubSpot, and Freshsales. It bundles contract lifecycle management, billing, and subscription management into a single platform. It handles pricing scenarios that most mid-market companies will never encounter.

That breadth is the problem.

HubSpot is not DealHub's primary focus. The integration model reflects it. DealHub creates custom objects in HubSpot instead of using native ones. It syncs data one way instead of writing directly to HubSpot's own records. It demands administration that most mid-market RevOps teams cannot absorb. (For a broader look at [where HubSpot native quoting breaks down](INTERNAL_LINK: hubspot-cpq-limitations), see our full guide.)

The RevOps specialist at a construction technology company with 200 sales quoters put it simply after learning what DealHub requires in practice: "They basically have an admin just for DealHub, and that's not something that we are looking for." The company dropped DealHub from evaluation entirely.

At a mid-market government technology company, the story went further. The team had already implemented DealHub and was living with the consequences. "It is so convoluted," their RevOps leader explained. "The CFO is quite unhappy because we have to have an agency." Any configuration change required engaging a DealHub-specific consulting partner. The internal team could not self-serve.

These are not growing pains. DealHub was never the right tool for these teams. It was the most visible CPQ option in the HubSpot ecosystem, and the market offered few alternatives. That is changing.

## The Data Integrity Problem: Custom Objects and One-Way Syncs

**DealHub creates custom objects and syncs data one-way into HubSpot. Your CRM data is only as accurate as DealHub's translation layer, and any changes you make in HubSpot get overwritten.**

This is not a configuration issue. It is an architectural one. DealHub does not write to HubSpot's native quote or line item objects. It creates its own data structures in your HubSpot instance. The consequences cascade through everything your CRM touches.

At a government technology company processing 100 to 150 proposals per month, the breakdown looked like this: 90% of quotes generated by DealHub required manual correction. Recurring fees appeared as one-time charges. Contract start and end dates did not sync. The RevOps leader listed what broke: "I can't rely on revenue reporting. I can't rely on pipeline reporting. I can't forecast correctly. I can't get insight into the line items and discounting correctly."

The sales team's response was predictable. They stopped using DealHub. "They're going in and editing the PDF," the RevOps leader said. When reps bypass the CPQ and manually edit the output document, the tool is providing negative value.

The data problems did not stay in HubSpot. "Nothing is coming in as data. And then when it goes to NetSuite, nothing is correct in NetSuite." Bad CPQ data corrupted both the CRM and the ERP.

DealHub also created an incomplete custom object called "DealHub subscriptions" because it could not work with HubSpot's native subscription objects. The RevOps leader wanted to remove it: "I would like to rip that thing out." But deleting a custom object with data attached to active deals is not simple.

The one-way sync created another problem. "DealHub writes to the product table. It's a one-way sync from DealHub back to HubSpot. I could make changes, but they're gonna get overwritten on the next sync." HubSpot was no longer the source of truth for its own product catalog.

The cleanup: 12,000 line items needing manual review. None with start or end dates.

When CPQ writes to native HubSpot objects instead of custom ones, these problems do not exist. Data is available in HubSpot's native reporting without a translation layer. A mid-market managed services company recovered 30% margin after switching to native HubSpot quoting with formula-based pricing. The accuracy came from the architecture, not from better training.

## What 'HubSpot-Native' Actually Means for CPQ

Native and integrated are not the same thing. The distinction has real consequences for reporting, workflows, and day-to-day operations.

**Native CPQ (uses HubSpot objects):**
- Quotes are HubSpot quote objects
- Line items are HubSpot line items
- Deal amounts update automatically from quote data
- HubSpot workflows trigger on quote events (approval status, signature, expiration)
- Native reporting works out of the box (recurring revenue, pipeline, line item analysis)
- HubSpot permissions apply to quoting
- Reps never leave HubSpot

**Integrated CPQ (syncs to HubSpot):**
- Quotes live in an external system
- Custom objects are created in HubSpot as sync targets
- Data flows one direction (or creates conflicts when it flows both ways)
- Workflows cannot trigger on external quote events without middleware
- Reporting requires custom reports built on custom objects
- Separate permission model to manage
- Reps work in a different interface

The RevOps leader at the government technology company was explicit about what she wanted next: "I want to use the HubSpot quotes, the HubSpot line items." After living with custom objects and one-way syncs, native HubSpot objects were the requirement, not a preference.

The RevOps specialist at the construction technology company echoed it: "We would like to have something native within HubSpot, that integrates well with HubSpot."

Line items are HubSpot line items. Every workflow, report, and permission that works with native HubSpot data works with Quotivity data.
