# Quotivity — Ideal Customer Profile

---

## Firmographics

### Industry

**Primary industries** (strongest fit and proof points):
1. SaaS companies with complex pricing models (tiered, usage-based, bundled hardware + software subscriptions). Generally growth-stage.
2. Manufacturing — configure-to-order and assemble-to-order companies, plus their dealers and distributors. This is the growing focus.

**Secondary industries** (emerging fit, fewer proof points):
1. Managed IT and cloud services (validated by True North case study)
2. Healthcare SaaS (validated by Aptarro case study)

### Company Size

- **Employee count:** Growth-stage companies with growing sales teams. No strict employee floor or ceiling, but enough sales reps that per-seat licensing at ~$10K average TCV makes sense.
- **Revenue range:** Companies whose deal complexity has outpaced their quoting tools. Typically post-Series A or established mid-market.
- **Why this range:** Below a certain size, teams can get by with HubSpot's default CPQ or manual processes. Above a certain complexity threshold, they've already invested in enterprise tools like DealHub.

### Geography

- **HQ location:** No geographic restriction. Quotivity serves the HubSpot ecosystem globally.
- **Platform constraint:** Must be on HubSpot Sales Hub Professional or higher.

### Tech Stack Signals

- HubSpot Sales Hub Professional or Enterprise
- Stable ERP system in place (indicates operational maturity and a system Quotivity needs to complement)
- Currently using spreadsheets, HubSpot native CPQ, or a disconnected quoting tool for complex pricing

---

## Dream Customer Profile

A manufacturer, distributor, or dealer with a growing sales team that sells products with interdependencies and pricing beyond simple fixed amounts. They're on HubSpot Sales Hub Professional, have a stable ERP, and executive stakeholders are bought in to improving their quoting process. Their busy season is 3-4 months away, giving enough runway for implementation and adoption before volume spikes.

Their sales reps currently jump between spreadsheets and HubSpot to build quotes. Every quote needs manual approval because nobody trusts the numbers. Sales management is buried in administrative review. Finance has no way to control discounting except by looking at every quote. RevOps has been trying to make HubSpot's native quoting work but has hit a wall on templates and policy enforcement.

### Disqualifiers

- Not on HubSpot (or on HubSpot Starter / Free — Sales Hub Professional is required)
- Simple pricing that HubSpot's native CPQ handles fine (fixed prices, no bundles, no configuration)
- Already locked into DealHub or similar enterprise CPQ with a multi-year contract
- No executive buy-in for changing the quoting process
- No stable ERP (indicates operational immaturity that may create implementation friction)

## Buying Committee

### Economic Buyer / Decision Maker

- **Typical title:** VP of Sales, Sales Director
- **What they care about:** Quote velocity, rep productivity, reducing administrative approval burden, visibility into quoting activity
- **How they evaluate:** Impact on deal speed, ease of rep adoption, reduction in time spent on administrative quote review

### Champion (who drives the evaluation internally)

- **Typical title:** RevOps Leader, Sales Operations Manager
- **What they care about:** Template management, policy enforcement, ease of ongoing administration, integration with existing HubSpot workflows
- **How they evaluate:** Hands-on with the product. Can it replace their current patchwork of spreadsheets and workarounds? How much implementation work falls on them?

### Financial Influencer

- **Typical title:** Controller, Finance Director, CFO
- **What they care about:** Discount governance, margin integrity, reliable reporting on what's being quoted
- **How they evaluate:** Cost vs. value. Can this reduce the busy work of reviewing every quote for discounting issues?

## External Signals

### Trigger Events (strong buying signals)

1. Adopting HubSpot or migrating to HubSpot Sales Hub Professional / Enterprise
2. Growing their sales team (job postings for sales reps, AEs, sales managers)
3. Executive stakeholders vocal about improving sales operations or quoting
4. Busy season 3-4 months away (ideal implementation timing)
5. Hiring for RevOps roles (indicates investment in sales process maturity)

### Behavioral Signals (moderate buying signals)

1. Engaging with HubSpot CPQ limitation discussions in community forums
2. Searching for HubSpot quoting solutions in the HubSpot Marketplace
3. Posting about quoting pain or spreadsheet frustrations on LinkedIn
4. Attending HubSpot ecosystem events (INBOUND, HUGs)

### Negative Signals (deprioritize these accounts)

1. Not on HubSpot or evaluating other CRMs
2. Simple product catalog with fixed pricing (no configuration or pricing complexity)
3. Recently implemented DealHub or another enterprise CPQ
4. Small sales team (1-2 reps) where per-seat licensing economics don't work
