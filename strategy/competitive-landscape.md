# Quotivity — Competitive Landscape

---

## Market Overview

### Market Categories

1. **HubSpot CPQ / Quoting** — we compete as the native upgrade for companies that have outgrown HubSpot's default quoting
2. **Mid-Market CPQ** — we compete on HubSpot-native depth and speed-to-value against tools that live outside the CRM
3. **Status Quo Alternatives** — we compete against spreadsheets and ERP quoting, which are the most common "competitors" in practice

### Competitive Landscape Summary

The HubSpot quoting space is split between three tiers. HubSpot's native CPQ handles simple quoting but breaks down with complex pricing and configuration. Enterprise tools like DealHub handle the complexity but are expensive, slow to implement, and never fully native to HubSpot. In between, a handful of smaller players (Mobileforce, GleanQuote) offer quoting outside of HubSpot with limited integration.

The biggest competitor is the status quo: spreadsheets and ERP quoting systems. Most companies we talk to aren't comparing us to another CPQ tool. They're comparing us to the patchwork of spreadsheets, manual processes, and workarounds they've been using for years.

Quotivity occupies the gap: HubSpot-native, purpose-built for complex quoting, implementable in weeks, priced for mid-market teams.

## Competitor 1: HubSpot CPQ (Native Quoting)

### Company and Product

- **Company:** HubSpot
- **Product:** HubSpot Quotes (part of Sales Hub)
- **Pricing model:** Included with Sales Hub Professional and Enterprise
- **Market position:** Default quoting tool for every HubSpot customer. Rapid investment and evolution from HubSpot's product team.

### Positioning (How They Describe Themselves)

HubSpot positions native quoting as part of the all-in-one Sales Hub. Their messaging emphasizes simplicity, speed, and staying within the HubSpot ecosystem. They target teams that want basic quoting without adding another tool.

### Strengths (Be Honest)

1. **Already there** — every Sales Hub Pro customer has it. Zero additional cost, zero implementation.
2. **Rapid investment** — HubSpot is actively improving their quoting capabilities. The gap narrows with every release.
3. **Simplicity** — for companies with straightforward pricing, it works fine and there's no reason to switch.

### Weaknesses (Where We Genuinely Differ)

1. **Can't handle complex pricing** — no support for tiered pricing, formula-based pricing, or volume discounts beyond basic line-item math.
2. **No product configuration** — no way to define product interdependencies, required add-ons, or valid combinations.
3. **Limited quote customization** — templates are painful to customize and lack the flexibility RevOps teams need.
4. **Minimal policy enforcement** — no discount controls, no approval workflows based on pricing rules, no guardrails.

### Positioning Gaps

1. **Complex pricing and configuration** — the core gap. Any company with bundled products, tiered pricing, or product interdependencies hits a wall.
2. **Discount governance** — no way to set and enforce discount limits by role, product, or deal type.
3. **Template flexibility** — quote template customization is limited and frustrating for RevOps.

### Common Objections When They're in the Deal

1. "HubSpot's quoting is already included. Why would I pay for something else?"
2. "HubSpot keeps improving their quoting. Won't they catch up?"
3. "I don't want to add another tool to the stack."

**How to handle each:**
1. "HubSpot's quoting works for simple pricing. Show me your price book and I'll show you where it breaks." Demonstrate the specific gap with their actual pricing model.
2. "HubSpot's investment is real. But complex pricing and configuration is a different product category. HubSpot is building a better quoting tool. We've built a CPQ."
3. "Quotivity isn't another tool. It's inside HubSpot. Your reps don't leave the CRM. It's an upgrade, not an addition."

### Battlecard Summary

1. Don't bash HubSpot. Position Quotivity as an upgrade that keeps the full power of the platform. "HubSpot's quoting got you started. Quotivity scales your quoting when your pricing gets complex."
2. Make it tangible. Ask the prospect to walk through building a quote with bundled products or tiered pricing in HubSpot's native tool. The limitation sells itself.
3. Emphasize that Quotivity is HubSpot-exclusive. We're not hedging across CRMs. Our entire product is built for HubSpot depth.

## Competitor 2: DealHub

### Company and Product

- **Company:** DealHub
- **Product:** DealHub CPQ, DealRoom
- **Pricing model:** Enterprise pricing, typically high five figures to six figures annually
- **Market position:** Mature enterprise CPQ with broad capability set. Strong in complex, multi-product enterprises.

### Positioning (How They Describe Themselves)

DealHub positions as a "revenue platform" that unifies CPQ, CLM, billing, and subscription management. Their messaging emphasizes handling extreme complexity and being a complete revenue workflow solution.

### Strengths (Be Honest)

1. **Maturity** — they've been solving complex quoting for years. Feature set is deep.
2. **Extreme complexity support** — if you need enterprise-grade CPQ with every edge case covered, DealHub can do it.
3. **Broader platform** — CLM, billing, and subscription management in addition to CPQ.

### Weaknesses (Where We Genuinely Differ)

1. **Expensive** — pricing puts it out of reach for most mid-market teams, especially those comparing against HubSpot's free native quoting.
2. **Long implementation** — typical implementations take months. Companies facing an upcoming busy season can't wait.
3. **Incomplete HubSpot integration** — DealHub works with HubSpot but is not native to it. Data lives in two places. The experience is a bolt-on, not integrated.

### Positioning Gaps

1. **HubSpot-native experience** — DealHub pulls reps out of HubSpot. Quotivity keeps them in it.
2. **Speed to value** — weeks vs. months for implementation.
3. **Mid-market fit** — DealHub is built for enterprises. The complexity of the tool matches the complexity of their pricing — which is overkill for most mid-market teams.

### Common Objections When They're in the Deal

1. "DealHub can do more than Quotivity."
2. "We need a tool that scales with us as we grow."
3. "DealHub integrates with HubSpot."

**How to handle each:**
1. "DealHub can do more. The question is whether you need it, and whether you want to pay for it and wait months to implement it. Most mid-market teams on HubSpot don't."
2. "Quotivity scales with your pricing complexity. What it won't do is pull your sales team out of HubSpot. If HubSpot is your CRM, your quoting should live there."
3. "Integrates with and native to are different things. Ask how deal data flows, where reps build quotes, and what happens to your HubSpot workflows."

### Battlecard Summary

1. "If your team is trying to bring sales reps into HubSpot, there is one answer: Quotivity. We get your team up and running quickly."
2. Force the implementation timeline conversation. "When is your next busy season? Can you wait 6 months for a CPQ rollout?"
3. Make the HubSpot-native distinction tangible. "Where do your reps build quotes? In HubSpot, or in another tab?"

3. **No complex bundle and configuration support** — cannot define product interdependencies or required add-ons.

### Positioning Gaps

1. **HubSpot-native experience** — Mobileforce is a separate tool with an integration, not a native HubSpot experience.
2. **Complex pricing and configuration** — the core capability gap.

### Battlecard Summary

1. "Mobileforce is built for field service. Quotivity is built for HubSpot."
2. If the prospect is on HubSpot, the conversation is short: "Where does your sales team work? In HubSpot. Where should your quoting live? In HubSpot."

## Competitor 5: Excel Spreadsheets (Status Quo)

### Why This Is a Competitor

Excel is the most common "competitor" in practice. Most prospects aren't comparing Quotivity to another CPQ. They're comparing it to the spreadsheet-based process they've been using for years.

### Strengths (Be Honest)

1. **Familiar** — everyone knows how to use Excel. No onboarding required.
2. **Flexible** — can model any pricing scenario with enough formulas.
3. **Free** — no additional software cost.

### Weaknesses

1. **Disconnected from everything** — best case, it gives you answers that need to be transferred to another system. Worst case, reps save pricing tables to their desktops and quote from stale data.
2. **Difficult to maintain and distribute** — pricing changes require updating and redistributing spreadsheets. Version control is nonexistent.
3. **No guardrails** — no approval workflows, no discount controls, no policy enforcement.
4. **No visibility** — management can't see what's being quoted until the deal closes.

### Battlecard Summary

1. Don't insult the spreadsheet. It got them this far. Acknowledge that it worked when things were simple.
2. Make the cost of the status quo concrete. "How many hours per week does your team spend building quotes in spreadsheets? How many quotes need rework because the pricing was wrong?"
3. Use the Aptarro story. "Their reps had Excel pricing tables saved to their desktops. Pricing varied between partners. After Quotivity: 6-day negotiation time, down from 29."

## Competitor 6: ERP Quoting (Status Quo)

### Why This Is a Competitor

Some prospects, especially in manufacturing, have quoting capabilities built into their ERP (NetSuite, SAP, etc.). They use the ERP for quoting because it's already there, even though it's not where the sales team works.

### Strengths (Be Honest)

1. **Already in place** — no additional purchase required.
2. **Connected to inventory and fulfillment** — ERP quoting can pull real-time inventory and lead times.

### Weaknesses

1. **Not where the sales team works** — reps work in the CRM (HubSpot). Quoting in the ERP means context-switching and disconnected data.
2. **Difficult and expensive to customize** — ERP customization is slow, costly, and often requires specialized consultants.
3. **Poor user experience for sales** — ERP interfaces are built for operations, not for reps who need to build and send quotes quickly.

### Battlecard Summary

1. Don't position against the ERP. Position Quotivity as the quoting layer that sits on top of HubSpot and complements the ERP.
2. "Your ERP handles fulfillment. HubSpot handles the sales process. Quotivity handles the quoting. Each system does what it's built for."
3. "How long does it take a rep to build a quote in your ERP? How does that compare to doing it inside the CRM where they already spend their day?"
