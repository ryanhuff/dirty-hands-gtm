# Quotivity — Voice Guide

---

## Voice Attributes

### Attribute 1: Direct

- **Definition:** Say what we mean in the fewest words possible. One idea per sentence. No hedging, no qualifiers, no filler.
- **Sounds like:**
  - "HubSpot's native CPQ can't handle complex pricing. Quotivity can."
  - "Reps spend 3 hours building a quote that should take 10 minutes. That's a process problem."
- **Does NOT sound like:**
  - "We believe that by providing a comprehensive quoting solution, we can help organizations significantly improve their overall sales efficiency."

### Attribute 2: Specific

- **Definition:** Use concrete examples, real numbers, and simple math instead of vague claims. If you can't put a number on it, describe the before and after.
- **Sounds like:**
  - "Aptarro cut negotiation time from 29 days to 6 days."
  - "True North recovered 30% margin by replacing estimated pricing with formula-based calculations."
- **Does NOT sound like:**
  - "Our customers see significant improvements in quoting speed and accuracy."

### Attribute 3: Inclusive

- **Definition:** Write for sales, RevOps, and finance at the same time. If a sentence only makes sense to one audience, rewrite it. The buying committee reads the same content.
- **Sounds like:**
  - "Reps quote faster. Finance trusts the numbers. RevOps stops patching workarounds."
  - "Discount controls that let reps move fast and keep margins intact."
- **Does NOT sound like:**
  - "Leveraging advanced CPQ configuration paradigms to optimize your revenue operations stack." (RevOps jargon that excludes sales and finance)

### Attribute 4: Lightly Humorous

- **Definition:** Situational humor that acknowledges the absurdity of broken quoting processes. Never at a customer's expense. Never forced.
- **Sounds like:**
  - "Make HubSpot quotes behave."
  - "Your sales team shouldn't need a PhD in spreadsheet archaeology to send a quote."
- **Does NOT sound like:**
  - "Say goodbye to quoting headaches forever! (party emoji)" (forced enthusiasm)
  - "Still using spreadsheets? Yikes." (punching down at the reader)

## Tone by Context

| Content Type | Tone Shift | Example |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| LinkedIn thought leadership | Confident, peer-to-peer. Lead with an insight or opinion, not a product pitch. | "Revenue leakage isn't a pricing problem. It's a process problem. When reps quote from spreadsheets saved to their desktops, errors compound silently." |
| Product / feature content | Clear, practical. Show what it does and why that matters. No hype. | "Price books let you set pricing rules once. Reps see the right price automatically. No spreadsheet lookup required." |
| Case studies | Let the customer's story lead. Numbers first, narrative second. | "Aptarro's reps were quoting from desktop spreadsheets. After Quotivity: 29 days to 6 days average negotiation time." |
| Sales emails | Conversational, specific to their situation. Not salesy. | "Saw your team just posted a RevOps role. If quoting complexity is part of why, worth a look at how we handle it natively in HubSpot." |
| Website copy | Concise, benefit-led. Tagline energy. | "Quotes with guardrails, inside HubSpot." |
| Help / UI text | Helpful, human, no personality needed. Clarity is the goal. | "You're missing a required input. Add it and we'll finish the quote." |

## Style Rules

### Sentence Structure
- Short to medium sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Lead with the insight, not the setup. No throat-clearing introductions.
- Vary rhythm. Short sentences for impact. Slightly longer ones for nuance. Never the same cadence for three sentences in a row.

### Data and Evidence
- Use concrete examples and simple math when possible. "29 days to 6 days" is better than "79% reduction" which is better than "significant improvement."
- Show both the absolute numbers and the percentage when you have them.
- Every claim needs a proof point. If you don't have one, cut the claim.

### Technical Language
- Only standard HubSpot and quoting terms. Define anything uncommon on first use.
- Spell out acronyms on first use: "Configure, Price, Quote (CPQ)."
- Never assume the reader knows what a term means just because your team uses it daily.

### Formatting
- Headers for scannability. No section should go more than 3 paragraphs without a visual break.
- Bullet points for lists of 3 or more items.
- Bold key phrases for scanning, but no more than one bold phrase per paragraph.

### Punctuation
- No em-dashes. Rewrite with periods, colons, commas, or parentheses.
- No semicolons in marketing content. If a sentence needs a semicolon, it's two sentences.

## Anti-Patterns

### Structural Anti-Patterns
- **Feature dumping:** Listing capabilities without connecting them to outcomes. Every feature mention needs a "which means..." for the reader.
- **HubSpot bashing:** We complement HubSpot. Position as an upgrade, not a replacement. Never trash the platform our customers chose.
- **Complexity theater:** Making quoting sound harder than it is to justify the product. Our job is to simplify, and our content should reflect that.
- **Audience exclusion:** Writing only for RevOps or only for sales leaders. Every piece should be accessible to the full buying committee.
- **Leading with the product:** The reader should nod at the problem before they see our name. Lead with the insight, not the pitch.

### Phrase Anti-Patterns
- Never open with "In today's..." or any variation.
- Never say "We're excited to announce..." Just announce it.
- Never use "unlock," "empower," or "drive" as verbs.
- Never describe Quotivity as "the leading" or "the premier" anything.
- Never say "seamless integration." Say what it connects to and how.

### Tone Anti-Patterns
- Never be self-deprecating about the product. Confidence, not arrogance.
- Never talk down to the reader.
- Never use humor that requires explaining.
- Never make jokes at a customer's expense.

## Prohibited Terms

| Prohibited | Use Instead | Reason |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| "Leverage" (as verb) | "Use" | Corporate jargon |
| "Utilize" | "Use" | Same word, more syllables |
| "Seamless" | Describe the actual experience | Overused filler |
| "Robust" | Say what makes it durable or complete | Meaningless without specifics |
| "Cutting-edge" | Describe the specific capability | Empty modifier |
| "Revolutionary" | Describe the actual change | Hyperbole |
| "Game-changer" | State the measurable impact | Hype word |
| "Next-generation" | Say what's different | Vague |
| "Best-in-class" | Provide the evidence | Unsubstantiated superlative |
| "World-class" | Provide the evidence | Unsubstantiated superlative |
| "Synergy" | Describe the combined effect | Buzzword |
| "Holistic" | Name the specific scope | Vague |
| "End-to-end" (as filler) | Describe the specific scope | Vague |
| "Paradigm shift" | Describe the specific change | Dated and pretentious |
| "Disruptive" | Describe how you're different | Overused |
| "Bleeding edge" | Describe what's new | Hype |
| "Streamline" | Say what step was removed or time saved | Vague without specifics |
| "Optimize" | Say what improved and by how much | Vague without specifics |
| "Very" / "really" / "quite" | Remove entirely | Weak qualifiers |
| "Basically" / "essentially" | Remove entirely | Filler words |
| "AI-powered" | Describe what the automation does | Triggers skepticism |
| "Effortless" | Describe the actual experience | Nothing about CPQ is effortless |

## Example Rewrites

### Rewrite 1: Removing Corporate Bloat

**Before:**
> "Quotivity leverages cutting-edge technology to provide a seamless, end-to-end quoting experience that empowers sales teams to optimize their workflow and drive revenue growth."

**After:**
> "Quotivity puts pricing rules, product configuration, and approvals inside HubSpot. Reps build accurate quotes without leaving their CRM. Finance controls discounting without reviewing every quote."

**What changed:** Removed six prohibited terms. Replaced vague claims with specific capabilities and outcomes. Split one bloated sentence into three clear ones.

### Rewrite 2: Making the Problem Concrete

**Before:**
> "Many companies struggle with inefficient quoting processes that lead to suboptimal outcomes and missed revenue opportunities."

**After:**
> "Aptarro's reps quoted from spreadsheets saved to their desktops. Pricing varied between partners. Every quote needed manual approval. After Quotivity: 6-day average negotiation time, down from 29."

**What changed:** Replaced abstract language with a specific customer story. Added the numbers. Made the problem and the outcome feel real.

### Rewrite 3: Writing for the Full Buying Committee

**Before:**
> "Our advanced CPQ configuration engine enables revenue operations teams to implement sophisticated pricing logic and approval hierarchies."

**After:**
> "RevOps sets the pricing rules. Reps see the right price automatically. Finance gets discount controls that enforce themselves. One setup, three teams served."

**What changed:** Translated a RevOps-only sentence into language all three personas understand. Showed the outcome for each role instead of describing the technical mechanism.
